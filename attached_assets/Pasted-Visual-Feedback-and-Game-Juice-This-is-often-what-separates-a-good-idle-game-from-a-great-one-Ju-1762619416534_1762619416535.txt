Visual Feedback and "Game Juice"
This is often what separates a good idle game from a great one. "Juice" refers to all the little visual and audio cues that make the game feel alive and responsive.

Make Clicks Satisfying: When the "Click Boost!" is active, make it obvious!

Have the button itself pulse or glow.

Add a particle effect (like a flash or a "whoosh") that emanates from the central time machine with every click.

Show "floating text" (e.g., +10 ChronoCoins!) that pops up and fades away.

Animate the Machine: The central time machine is your main "character."

Give it a constant, subtle idle animation, like a slow pulse, a hover, or spinning rings.

When it "serves" a customer, make it play a more dramatic animation (e.g., it spins faster, a light flashes).

Reward Upgrades Visually: This is a huge motivator for players.

When the "Time Machine Level" (from the Upgrades tab) increases, consider adding a new visual element to the machine. Maybe it gets a new glowing ring, a different color, or a new spinning part. This makes the player feel their progress, they don't just see it in a number.

2. UI/UX Clarity and Readability
Your layout is good, but a few tweaks could make it easier for a new player to understand instantly.

Emphasize Key Numbers: The most important number on the screen is "ChronoCoins." Consider making that font slightly larger or bolder than the "Waiting," "Sending," and "Served" stats so the player's eye is drawn right to their earnings.

Add Icons: The tabs at the bottom ("Upgrades," "Managers," etc.) are clear, but adding simple icons next to the text (e.g., a gear for Upgrades, a person silhouette for Managers, a map for Destinations) can make the UI scannable even faster.

Clarify Bar Functions: It's not immediately obvious what happens when the three main progress bars (orange, blue, green) fill up.

Consider adding a simple tooltip that appears if the player hovers over them.

Alternatively, you could add a label like "Payout: 1.0K" instead of "Next At: 1K" (if that's what it does) to make the reward more explicit.

3. Theme and Aesthetic
You have a cool "Chrono" theme! Lean into it.

Evolve the "Customers": The simple "meeple" figures are great and clear. As a later-game feature, you could have different types of customers start appearing. Maybe after prestiging, you start getting figures that look like cavemen, or robots, or knights, which could be worth more ChronoCoins.

Polish the UI Elements: The current UI is very functional with flat colors. You could make it feel more "designed" with subtle changes:

Adding slight gradients to the progress bars.

Using rounded corners on the buttons and tabs.

Adding a thin, glowing border to the different UI panels to make them pop from the dark background.

Background Visuals: The dark background is clean, but it's also a big empty space. You could add a very slow, subtle animation to it, like a swirling time vortex or faint, passing stars. This adds atmosphere without distracting from the main gameplay.
