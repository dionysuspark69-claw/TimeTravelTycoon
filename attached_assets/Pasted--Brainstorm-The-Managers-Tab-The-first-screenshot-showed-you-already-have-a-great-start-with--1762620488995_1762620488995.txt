🧠 Brainstorm: The "Managers" Tab
The first screenshot showed you already have a great start with "Customer Recruiter," "Speed Technician," and "Revenue Accountant." Let's expand on that!

In idle games, "Managers" are fantastic for two things: automation and powerful, unique boosts that are different from simple upgrades.

Core Idea: Instead of just being a flat passive bonus (which the "Upgrades" tab already does well), Managers should have unlockable perks and special abilities as you level them up. This makes leveling them feel like a major event.

Here's a possible structure:

Customer Recruiter (Let's call him "Dr. S. Treamline")

Base Effect: Attracts customers 10% faster.

Level 10 Unlock: "Temporal Beacon"

Ability: Every 60 seconds, a "wave" of 2x the normal amount of customers instantly appears. This makes the game feel spikey and exciting.

Level 25 Unlock: "VIP Clients"

Ability: 1% chance for any customer to be a "VIP" (maybe they are gold-colored?) who pays 100x the normal ChronoCoins.

Speed Technician (Let's call her "Jenna 'Warp' Ryker")

Base Effect: Time machine travels 10% faster.

Level 10 Unlock: "Slipstream"

Ability: After a trip, the machine has a 50% chance to instantly return (0 travel time back), letting it pick up the next group immediately.

Level 25 Unlock: "Overclock" (Active Ability Button)

Ability: A new button appears on the main screen. When clicked, all trips are instantaneous for 10 seconds. (Has a 5-minute cooldown).

Revenue Accountant (Let's call him "Paradox Paulie")

Base Effect: Increases revenue from each trip by 10%.

Level 10 Unlock: "Compound Interest"

Ability: For every 100 trips completed, the base revenue for all trips permanently increases by 1%. This rewards long-term play.

Level 25 Unlock: "Time-Share Scam"

Ability: 5% chance any trip pays out 3x revenue (as if you "sold" the trip to customers from three different eras).

🚀 Brainstorm: The "Destinations" Tab
This is the perfect use of your time-travel theme! "Destinations" can act as new "worlds" or "levels" that change the game's mechanics and multipliers.

Core Idea: Unlocking new Destinations (time periods) costs a large amount of ChronoCoins. Traveling to a new Destination changes the "customers" and applies new Pros & Cons, adding a layer of strategy.

You could have a dropdown or map on this tab to select your Active Destination.

Destination 1: "The Present" (Default)

Cost: Unlocked

Visuals: The "meeple" customers you have now.

Effect: Base 1x multipliers. The standard experience.

Destination 2: "The Stone Age"

Cost: 1 Million ChronoCoins

Visuals: Customers become little cavemen meeple. The background could change to a prehistoric jungle.

Pro: "Easily Impressed" — Customers pay +50% ChronoCoins per trip.

Con: "Primitive Routes" — Time machine speed is -20%.

Destination 3: "The Far Future"

Cost: 1 Billion ChronoCoins

Visuals: Customers become little robot meeple. The starfield background gets more "cyberpunk" or complex.

Pro: "Advanced Tech" — Time machine speed is +50%.

Con: "Jaded Tourists" — Customers pay -20% ChronoCoins per trip.

Destination 4: "The Jurassic Period"

Cost: 1 Trillion ChronoCoins

Visuals: Customers are little explorers in safari hats. A T-Rex head graphic could occasionally appear.

Pro: "High-Risk, High-Reward" — Customers pay +500% ChronoCoins per trip.

Con: "It's Dangerous!" — -50% Customer Generation (they're scared!) and a 10% chance a trip "fails" (e.g., T-Rex attack) and pays zero coins.

This system makes the "Destinations" tab a strategic choice. A player might ask, "Are my Speed upgrades high enough to make 'The Stone Age' worth it?" or "Is my Customer Generation high enough to profit in 'The Jurassic Period'?"he "Prestige" system is the meta-loop. It's for when the player hits a "wall"—when upgrades become so expensive that progress slows to a crawl. The player can then voluntarily reset their main progress in exchange for a powerful new currency.

Here's a brainstorm for how it could work with your "Chrono" theme.

🌌 Brainstorm: The "Prestige" System
Your "Prestige" button could be called "Create New Timeline" or "Initiate Paradox."

The Core Loop
Reaching the "Wall": The player grinds and buys upgrades until they have a massive amount of ChronoCoins (e.g., 1 Quadrillion). The "Prestige" button, which was previously greyed out, now glows and shows them what they'll earn.

The "Prestige": The player clicks the button. A big warning appears:

"Are you sure you want to create a new timeline? This will reset all of your ChronoCoins, Upgrades, and Managers, but you will gain 150 Temporal Essence!"

The Reset: The player confirms. The game resets to the very beginning, but with a few key differences:

All ChronoCoins are gone.

All "Upgrades" (Time Machine Level, Capacity, etc.) are back to Level 1.

All "Managers" are back to Level 0/unhired.

All "Destinations" are re-locked (this makes re-playing more fun).

BUT... The player now has 150 Temporal Essence, a new currency.

AND... All "Achievements" remain unlocked.

The New Currency: "Temporal Essence"
What does this "Temporal Essence" (or Time Crystals, Paradox Shards, etc.) do? Two things:

A Permanent Global Boost: For every piece of Temporal Essence you own, all ChronoCoin earnings are permanently increased by +1%.

This is the most critical part. The player's second run is now 150% faster than their first. Their third run might be 500% faster, etc. This makes the grind feel super fast and rewarding each time you reset.

A New, Permanent Upgrade Shop: This is the real hook. Clicking the "Prestige" button (or a new tab that appears) now also opens a new upgrade shop where you can only spend Temporal Essence. These upgrades are PERMANENT and never reset.

The "Temporal Upgrades" (The REAL Hook)
This new shop is where you put the most powerful, game-changing upgrades. For example:

"Head Start" (Costs 10 Essence): Start every new timeline with 1,000 ChronoCoins.

"Managerial Contracts" (Costs 50 Essence): All Managers start at Level 1 automatically.

"Chrono-Boost" (Costs 100 Essence): The boost from Temporal Essence is now +1.5% per Essence (instead of +1%). (This is an upgrade for your prestige currency!)

"Instant Gratification" (Costs 200 Essence): The "Click Boost!" button is automatically active for the first 60 seconds of every new timeline.

"Time-Skip" (Costs 500 Essence): Start every new timeline with 1 hour's worth of your previous timeline's earnings.

"Permanent Destination" (Costs 1,000 Essence): "The Stone Age" destination is permanently unlocked from the start of every timeline.

This system gives the player a fantastic "new game plus" loop. The game isn't just about getting ChronoCoins; it's about getting enough ChronoCoins to reset for more Temporal Essence, so you can buy more permanent upgrades, to get more ChronoCoins... faster.. 📜 A Quest / Mission System
Right now, your "Achievements" tab is great for rewarding passive milestones (like the "First Voyage"). You can add an active "Quests" or "Missions" tab to give players short-term, active goals.

How it works: The tab shows 3-5 active missions at a time. When one is completed, a new one takes its place.

Examples:

"Time Rush: Earn 10,000 ChronoCoins in 60 seconds."

"Frequent Flyer: Complete 50 trips."

"Managerial: Hire a 'Speed Technician'."

"Click-Happy: Activate 'Click Boost!' 3 times."

Reward: Completing a mission could grant a nice bundle of ChronoCoins, or even a small amount of the "Temporal Essence" prestige currency! This gives players a way to earn prestige currency before they're ready to fully reset.

2. 🏺 Temporal Artifacts (A Collection System)
This is a huge hook for idle games. It adds a "gacha" or "collector" element that keeps people playing for a very long time.

How it works: Every time your machine completes a trip, there is a very, very small chance (e.g., 0.1%) that it brings back a "Temporal Artifact" from that Destination.

The Artifacts:

From "The Stone Age": You could find a "Perfectly Chipped Arrowhead," a "Fossilized Meeple," or a "Cave Painting Fragment."

From "The Far Future": You could find a "Miniature Fusion Core," a "Holographic Pet," or a "Sentient Nanobot."

The Reward (Two-Fold):

Unique Boost: Each new artifact you collect gives a small, permanent global boost (e.G., "+0.5% All ChronoCoin Gains").

Set Bonus: Collecting all 5 artifacts from "The Stone Age" might give a bigger, specific bonus (e.g., "Stone Age trips are 2x faster"). This encourages players to "farm" specific destinations.

3. 🎨 The "Workshop" (Time Machine Customization)
You have a beautiful centerpiece for your game. Let players make it their own!

How it works: A new tab called "Workshop" or "Garage" where players can spend currency (either late-game ChronoCoins or prestige-level Temporal Essence) to buy cosmetics.

What can they customize?

Skins/Paint Jobs: Change the time machine from its base green to "Carbon Fiber Black," "Gold Plated," "Retro '80s," etc.

Trail Effects: Change the visual "whoosh" effect it leaves behind. (e.g., a rainbow trail, a fire trail, a glitchy "matrix" trail).

Customer Skins: Maybe you can unlock different "meeple" styles (like changing them all to look like aliens, zombies, or little knights).

4. 🌀 Time-Limited Events ("Temporal Anomalies")
This is a classic way to keep the game fresh and create urgency.

How it works: Every weekend (or for one week a month), a "Temporal Anomaly" event happens.

Examples:

"The Gold Rush": A new, temporary destination appears: "1849 California." It has massive ChronoCoin payouts but very slow customer generation.

"Manager Madness": For 48 hours, all Manager perks and abilities are 2x as effective.

"Prestige-a-Palooza": For one day, prestiging gives +25% more Temporal Essence.
